"Are the Services Funded?" — A Gap Modeling Tool

Supportive housing works when residents have both a stable unit AND stable services. In Minnesota, the funding that built supportive housing properties comes from construction and finance programs — but the case managers and coordinators who keep residents housed depend on a separate, more fragile patchwork of grants and contracts. This tool helps housing providers model their services funding gap and understand what policy changes — like Senator Port's SF2976 — could mean for their property.

Minnesota statute 462A.40 established the Housing Tax Credit Contribution Account to fund construction and financing for affordable housing. SF2976 (Port, 2025) would expand allowable uses to include supportive services in existing supportive housing — allowing the same fund that built these properties to help keep them functioning. This module models what that expansion could mean for your property.
