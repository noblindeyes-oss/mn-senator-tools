SCHEMATIC — NOT TO SCALE. U.S. Highway 212 cross-section at CSAH 51, Benton Township, Carver County.

Highway 212 carries more daily truck traffic than the total traffic volume on 40% of Minnesota highways. At U.S. 212 and CSAH 51 in Carver County, that traffic collides at an at-grade intersection with a crash rate above the state average. Senator Gruenhagen's SF165 proposes $6 million to fix it. Authored — Complete
Senator Glenn H. Gruenhagen (SD-17) is the chief author of SF165. The bill was filed for the 94th Legislature, 2025 session. Authoring a bonding bill means the senator has formally submitted the request and taken public ownership of the project.
Introduced — Complete
SF165 was introduced in the 2025 session. Introduction means the bill has been read on the Senate floor and assigned to committee. It is now part of the official legislative record and can proceed through the committee process.
Committee — Pending
SF165 has been referred to the Energy, Utilities, Environment, and Climate Committee and the Capital Investment Committee. In committee, the bill will be reviewed, debated, and potentially amended before a vote to recommend it to the full Senate. Bonding bills must pass through the Capital Investment Committee to be included in the state bonding package.
Floor Vote — Pending
If the bill passes committee, it goes to the full Senate for a floor vote. Bonding bills in Minnesota require a three-fifths supermajority (40 of 67 senators) because they authorize the state to issue debt. This higher threshold means bonding projects typically need bipartisan support.
Governor Signature — Pending
After passing both chambers, the bonding bill goes to the Governor for signature. If signed, the $6 million appropriation from state bond proceeds is authorized and funds can flow to Carver County for the interchange project.
Construction — Project-Ready
Carver County Public Works has existing engineering design for the grade-separated quadrant interchange. The project shifts Highway 51 east, builds a new bridge carrying 51 over 212, and adds loop ramps for turning movements. Once funded, the county can proceed to construction without a lengthy design phase. This is a shovel-ready project.

Highway 212 is the spine. East of Norwood Young America, it carries Twin Cities commuters who moved to Carver County for affordable housing and now navigate a road network built for a fraction of today's population. The CSAH 51 intersection sits at the fault line between those two traffic worlds.

SF165 — Highway 212 / CSAH 51 Interchange

Project: Carver County State-Aid Highway 51 and U.S. Highway 212 intersection safety improvements

Location: Benton Township, Carver County, Minnesota

Bond Amount: $6,000,000 from state bond proceeds

Chief Author: Senator Glenn H. Gruenhagen (SD-17)

Session: 94th Legislature, 2025–2026

Project Description

Construction of a grade-separated quadrant interchange at the intersection of Carver County State-Aid Highway 51 and U.S. Highway 212. Highway 51 is shifted east, a new bridge carries 51 over 212, and loop ramps handle turning movements. This eliminates the at-grade conflict point that drives the crash problem at this location.

Key Data Points

  • Daily Traffic: ~12,500 vehicles/day on Hwy 212 (Carver County segment); projected to exceed 17,000/day by 2040
  • Freight Volume: 1,900+ trucks/day on Hwy 212 west of Chaska
  • Crash Rate: CSAH 51 / Hwy 212 intersection crash rate rated above state average by MnDOT and Carver County Public Works
  • Nearby: County Road 43 intersection crash rate also above state average
  • McLeod Freight: ~20% of Hwy 212 freight trips originate in McLeod County (3M Hutchinson)

Current Status

Authored and introduced. Referred to Energy, Utilities, Environment, and Climate Committee and Capital Investment Committee. Carver County has existing engineering design; project is construction-ready upon funding.
