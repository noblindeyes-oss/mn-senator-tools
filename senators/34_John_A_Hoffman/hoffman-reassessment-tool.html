Senate District 34  ·  Minn. Stat. § 256B.0911  ·  SF2598 (2025)
Senator Hoffman's SF2598 created a new pathway for abbreviated annual reassessments for Minnesotans with stable disability waiver service needs — reducing repetitive paperwork for families and case managers alike. This tool helps county case managers and care coordinators in Senate District 34 determine whether an annual disability waiver reassessment qualifies for the abbreviated pathway or requires a full in-person MnCHOICES visit. SD-34 includes communities in Anoka and Hennepin counties — Champlin, Brooklyn Park, Coon Rapids, Dayton, and Rogers — where county case managers coordinate waiver services for hundreds of individuals with disabilities. Anoka County is a primary partner for MnCHOICES assessments in this district.

Statute & Policy Reference

Section 256B.0911 governs long-term care consultation services in Minnesota, including the MnCHOICES assessment process used to determine eligibility for and planning of disability waiver services.

"At the time of reassessment, persons receiving waiver residential supports and services must be assessed to determine whether they would prefer alternative housing and service options through a person-centered planning process."
Cite: Minn. Stat. § 256B.0911, subd. 3a (or applicable subdivision). This housing-preference assessment component cannot be abbreviated — it must be conducted in person for persons in residential settings.

SF2598, filed March 13, 2025 by Senator John Hoffman (SD-34), amends Minn. Stat. § 256B.0911 to authorize abbreviated annual reassessments for qualifying individuals enrolled in disability waiver programs.

"Persons age 21 or older and under 65 may attest to unchanged needs for up to two consecutive reassessments, allowing abbreviated reassessments rather than requiring a full in-person MnCHOICES visit each year."
Cite: 2025 Minn. Laws, SF2598, amending § 256B.0911. This provision reduces the annual in-person burden for individuals with stable, well-documented disability service needs.

The bill applies to persons enrolled in the DD, CAC, CADI, and BI waiver programs. CFSS (Community First Services and Supports) is a non-waiver program and uses a different authorization framework.

Separate from the new abbreviated pathway, Minnesota statute already provides a remote reassessment option for certain waiver enrollees.

"Remote reassessments may be substituted for four consecutive reassessments if followed by an in-person reassessment, for persons on DD, CADI, CAC, and BI waivers."
Cite: Minn. Stat. § 256B.0911, as of 2025. Note: the remote option and the abbreviated pathway are separate provisions. The abbreviated pathway (SF2598) applies to the scope of the assessment; the remote option applies to the modality of delivery.

The 2025 legislative session added a verbal confirmation option that simplifies the signature requirement at reassessment.

"At reassessment, instead of a written signature, individuals may verbally confirm agreement with the assessment."
Cite: 2025 session legislative update; see also Minnesota Council on Disability guidance. This provision is relevant to the abbreviated pathway eligibility check — the person (or their legal representative) must be able to provide verbal confirmation of unchanged needs.

SF2598 exists within a broader waiver reform effort under the Waiver Reimagine initiative.

"The Waiver Reimagine Advisory Task Force was created in 2025 to provide stakeholder input on further changes to Minnesota's four disability waiver programs (DD, CAC, CADI, BI). Full implementation of Waiver Reimagine is delayed until January 1, 2027."
Cite: 2025 Minn. Laws; DHS Waiver Reimagine page (mn.gov/dhs). SF2598 and the co-authored Waiver Reimagine framework (SF3757, Senator Hoffman and Rep. Hicks) are among the near-term changes ahead of broader 2027 implementation.

Senate District 34 spans communities in both Anoka and Hennepin counties. Anoka County is the primary county partner for waiver case management in this district.

"Anoka County is one of SD-34's primary county partners for waiver case management. Case managers in Anoka County are certified assessors or work alongside certified assessors to complete MnCHOICES assessments for waiver-enrolled individuals."
Informational note — not statutory. For Anoka County-specific policy and procedures, contact Anoka County Human Services or visit the county website. Brooklyn Park and portions of Champlin also involve Hennepin County Human Services.
