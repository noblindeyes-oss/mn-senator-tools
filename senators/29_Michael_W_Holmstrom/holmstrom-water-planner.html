The MN Drinking Water State Revolving Fund (DWRF) is administered jointly by the MN Public Facilities Authority and MN Dept. of Health. Unlike USDA rural programs, it has no income-limit eligibility threshold — making it the primary debt vehicle for fast-growing Wright County cities that exceed USDA median household income caps.

SD-29 Context

Wright County grew from 141,337 (2020 Census) to approximately 154,593 by mid-2024 — roughly 10.8% in four years, the fastest county growth rate in Minnesota.

Senator Holmstrom's bonding priorities include $15M for Howard Lake (SF2582), $10M for Monticello (SF2223), and additional requests totaling $37.5M for the 2026 cycle.

SD-29 Case Studies
MONTICELLO (pop. ~14,000)
Received $11M state bonding in 2023 for new water treatment facility. Holmstrom's 2026 priority adds $10M more (SF2223). Total project financed partly through ~$32M+ MPFA loan. Demonstrates that large growth-city projects require multiple funding cycles over multiple years.
SOUTH HAVEN (cautionary case)
December 2025 Capital Investment Committee site visit documented bacterial contamination in distribution system, with simultaneous failures in water tower, sanitary sewer, and storm sewer. A city that reached crisis before bonding was secured — the case for early planning.
HOWARD LAKE (pop. ~2,326)
SF2582 (2025): $15M for new well, trunk water-main upgrades, and water tower improvements. Manganese contamination was the documented water quality driver. Storage: 250,000-gal tower (1999) + 75,000-gal tower (1947).
MN Bonding Process
Capital Investment Committee site visits: ongoing
Governor's bonding proposal: January (even year)
Legislative hearings: February–April (even year)
Floor vote (3/5 supermajority): May (even year)
Governor signs / MMB releases proceeds: June–August
A failed bonding bill = 2-year wait for next opportunity.
