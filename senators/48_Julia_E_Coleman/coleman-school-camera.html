Step 4 — Privacy Compliance Checklist

SF4906 includes specific data-privacy requirements. Walk through each one.

Requirement 1: Facial Image Masking by Default

Does your proposed analytics system employ facial image masking by default for general monitoring, with masking disabled only for documented security purposes under written policy?

Requirement 2: Facial Recognition Scope Limitation

If facial recognition is used, is it limited to narrowly defined, high-risk watch lists governed by written policy approved by district administration?

Requirement 3: State and Federal Privacy Law

Has your district confirmed that the proposed system complies with FERPA, MN Government Data Practices Act, and applicable state education data law?

Requirement 4: Data Retention Policy

Does your district have a written policy governing how long video analytics data is retained and who has access to it?

Requirement 5: Existing Infrastructure Priority

Does your application prioritize the use of existing camera infrastructure rather than purchasing new camera hardware?

SF4906 explicitly restricts grants from funding new camera hardware "except where necessary to integrate with existing camera systems." A primarily new-hardware application is unlikely to be competitive.
