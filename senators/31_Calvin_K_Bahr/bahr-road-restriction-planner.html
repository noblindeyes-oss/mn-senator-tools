Three agencies control road restrictions in north Anoka County. Each sets its own posting dates. Here is who controls what.
Layer 1
State
MnDOT controls trunk highways: TH-65, TH-10, TH-47, and other numbered state routes.
Posting by frost zone. Anoka County = Metro zone. Dates announced 3 days ahead.
Standard restriction: 5-ton or 7-ton single-axle on posted routes. Window: mid-March to mid-May.
OSOW permits: online at MnDOT.
Phone: 651-366-5400 | dot.mn.gov/loadlimits
Layer 2
County
Anoka County controls CSAHs and County Roads: CSAH 22, CSAH 54, CSAH 116 (Bunker Lake Blvd), CR 60, CR 61.
Posting date: March 3 at 12:01 AM (2025 and 2026; verify each year).
Oversize/overweight loads require a Transportation Permit (online only).
Key project: TH-65 / Bunker Lake Blvd interchange -- grade separation underway (~$46.8M). SF2145 funded frontage road realignment.
Phone: 763-324-3100 | anokacountymn.gov/317
Layer 3
City
Cities & Townships set their own street postings.
Ham Lake: all city streets default to 5-ton unless otherwise posted. Winter increases expire March 3 at 12:01 AM.
East Bethel, Nowthen, Ramsey: check each city directly. Township roads (Linwood, etc.): call county for routing.
Ham Lake: hamlakemn.gov

OSOW Permit Quick Facts

Single Trip Permit: Valid 7 days. $15 base + Damage Assessment Fee.
Annual Permit: Available for repetitive moves on established routes.
Apply: dot.state.mn.us/cvo/oversize/index.html

Before You Run
  • Check MnDOT load limits line or website for state highway status
  • Check Anoka County Transportation for county road posting status
  • Check city website if running city streets
  • Confirm OSOW permit is valid and route-approved if load exceeds legal limits
  • Spring restrictions can be posted with only 3 days' notice
  • Winter weight increases expire at 12:01 AM on posting date -- not end of business day
