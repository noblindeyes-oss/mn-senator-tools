Clean Energy Employment

Statewide

  • 62,102 Minnesotans employed in clean energy and clean vehicle sectors (Clean Jobs Midwest 2024)
  • 17,251 of those jobs are in Greater Minnesota (30% of total)
  • 57,000+ jobs in the clean energy sector specifically

Greater MN / Mankato Region

  • Clean energy job growth in Mankato exceeded 10% year-over-year — more than double the statewide average (Clean Jobs Midwest data)
  • Over 81% of MN clean energy employers report difficulty hiring — workforce pipeline is the binding constraint, not capital

Data Centers — New Demand, New Obligation
  1. Data centers are locating in Minnesota partly because of the state's clean, reliable grid — the very infrastructure Frentz's energy policy built. Minnesota's grid reliability and clean energy trajectory make it attractive to companies with net-zero commitments.
  2. Frentz co-authored Minnesota's data center governance law requiring centers to invest in communities, guarantee high-wage jobs, and help meet the state's clean energy goals — not merely consume its benefits.
  3. A single large hyperscale data center can draw 100-500 MW of electricity (industry figure). Without governance, that new demand could set back the 2040 timeline by years. With it, data centers can be required to procure clean power directly.
