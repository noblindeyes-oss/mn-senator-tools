Legislative Context — SF2043 Work Group
The Minnesota Legislature's 2025 session established a Spoken Language Health Care Interpreter Work Group through SF2043 (chief author: Sen. Melissa H. Wiklund, SD-51). The 15-member group — which includes interpreters, LEP patients, health plans, health systems, interpreter agencies, MDH, DHS, training programs, and hospital representation — is developing recommendations on interpreter workforce, quality standards, and payment. Clinics that document their language access gaps and improvement efforts now will be better positioned to respond to any standards or requirements that emerge from the work group's report.
Minnesota Interpreter Roster
Minnesota maintains a voluntary statewide Spoken Language Health Care Interpreter Roster administered by the Minnesota Department of Health (MDH) under Minn. Stat. 144.058. Roster-listed interpreters have met minimum competency standards. When contracting with interpreter agencies or individual contractors, ask whether their interpreters hold MDH roster certification. Roster information: www.health.state.mn.us/facilities/providers/interpreter
