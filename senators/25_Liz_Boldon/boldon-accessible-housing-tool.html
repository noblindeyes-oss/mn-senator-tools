Accessible Housing Gap Calculator & Capital Funding Navigator

Senator Boldon's SF1838 secured $600,000 for Accessible Space, Inc. to rehabilitate low-income accessible housing units at two Rochester addresses — but documenting that kind of need precisely enough to support a capital request is hard for most organizations.

This tool helps housing advocates, nonprofit developers, and local government staff calculate their community's accessible housing gap, build a realistic funding stack, and generate a capital request narrative for state bonding requests, MHFA applications, and local housing plans.

Accessible Space, Inc. is a Minnesota-based 501(c)(3) that develops and manages supportive housing for people with physical disabilities, brain injuries, and age-related limitations. Their Rochester sites (1680 Eastwood Road SE and 3461 Kenosha Drive NW) serve low-income residents requiring accessible unit features to live independently.
