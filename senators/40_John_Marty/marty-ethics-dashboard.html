In 1993, Senator John Marty authored the law that banned lobbyist gifts to Minnesota public officials. In 2025, he introduced SF1316 to end per diem payments during regular session. This tool was built to make the accountability framework he has spent his career building visible to any citizen who wants to use it.

III. Per Diem Reform Explainer & Calculator

SF1316 — Per Diem Prohibition During Regular Session (94th Legislature, 2025–2026)

Full title: "Per diem payments prohibition during a regular session of the legislature." Introduced February 13, 2025 (Senate journal p. 373). Authors: Marty, Bahr, Putnam, Kupec, Rest. Referred to State and Local Government Committee.

  • Would prohibit per diem payments to legislators during regular session entirely
  • Restricts compensation to salary, health insurance, retirement contributions, and other compensation specifically authorized by statute
  • Members may still receive per diem when the legislature is not meeting in regular session
  • Effective date as introduced: July 1, 2025

What Is Legislative Per Diem?

Minnesota legislators receive a daily expense allowance (per diem) when the legislature is in session, in addition to their base salary. The per diem rate is set by the Legislative Salary Council under Minn. Stat. § 3.099. Per diem is paid for each day the legislature meets — floor sessions, committee hearings, and caucus days. During a typical regular session (January–May), this accumulates to a substantial supplement beyond salary.

Legislators who reside more than 50 miles from the State Capitol receive a higher per diem rate than those who live within 50 miles. The exact current rates are published by the Legislative Salary Council. Enter the current verified rate below (see Minn. Stat. § 3.099 or the Legislative Salary Council's current schedule at revisor.mn.gov/statutes/cite/3.099).
