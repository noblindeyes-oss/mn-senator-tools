Module 1 -- Home Cost Decomposition

"Can We Build More Homes?"

In southern Minnesota, the gap between what it costs to build a new home and what a working family can afford to buy one runs $50,000 to $100,000 or more. A significant portion of that gap comes from local regulatory costs that cities control directly: permit fees, hookup fees, park dedications, approval timelines, and inspection requirements. This tool quantifies those costs and models what happens to home prices when cities choose to reduce them.

Senator Draheim's SF969 helped fund workforce homeownership in Greater Minnesota -- but the deeper problem is that in communities like Madison Lake and Waseca, it costs more to build a home than most families can afford to buy one. This tool shows why, and what cities can do about it.
