"What Does Hosting the State Fair
Actually Cost Us?"

SF3068, authored by Sen. Clare Oumou Verbeten, is the first Minnesota law to require the State Agricultural Society to reimburse host cities for fair-related costs. The harder problem is what comes next: cities need to document those costs to collect. This tool was built to help them do exactly that.

The Minnesota State Fair draws approximately 1.9 million visitors over 12 days (1,940,869 in 2025; record: 2,126,551 in 2019).

The fairgrounds occupy 322 acres in Falcon Heights. Two-thirds of Falcon Heights is public land — the fairgrounds and the University of Minnesota's St. Paul campus — leaving the city with a structurally small tax base.

Falcon Heights has approximately 5,000 residents (4,984 per 2023 estimate).

The State Agricultural Society is a public corporation governed by Minnesota Statutes, chapter 37. Prior to SF3068, no statute required it to reimburse host cities for event-related service costs.

SF3068 was chief-authored by Sen. Clare Oumou Verbeten (SD-66) and introduced March 27, 2025. Companion bill: HF3099.

Post-event cleanup includes street sweeping, pothole and curb repair, signage replacement, and restoration of public areas. Costs often extend weeks beyond the event itself.

Statutory Basis

Minnesota Statutes, section 37.13, subdivision 1, as amended by 2025 Session Laws — requires the State Agricultural Society to reimburse host cities for public safety, fire, emergency medical services, traffic management, sanitation, and neighborhood cleanup costs incurred as a result of events held on the fairgrounds. (SF3068 / companion HF3099, 2025 session; chief author Sen. Clare Oumou Verbeten, SD-66.)
