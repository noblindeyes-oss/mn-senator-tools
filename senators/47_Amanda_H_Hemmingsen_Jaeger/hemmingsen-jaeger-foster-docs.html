About This Tool

Senator Hemmingsen-Jaeger's SF4337 directs $300,000 to pair social-service agencies with nonprofits to help noncitizen foster youth secure the documents their futures depend on. This tool was built for the people doing that work -- so that no deadline slips through a spreadsheet, and every kid has their papers before the window closes.

Reference Facts

  • Minnesota had approximately 10,000 youth in foster care in 2025 (DCYF)
  • SIJS applications must be filed before the youth's 21st birthday (USCIS)
  • Minnesota law (Northstar Care / Minn. Stat. § 260C.212) requires agencies to help youth obtain vital documents before leaving care
  • SF4337 (2025 session, chief author Hemmingsen-Jaeger) appropriates $300,000 in FY2027 for grants pairing social-service agencies with nonprofits to help noncitizen foster youth secure required documents
  • The Minnesota Department of Children, Youth, and Families (DCYF) administers foster care statewide
  • Free or reduced-fee birth certificates for homeless youth under 24 are available through the Minnesota Department of Health (651-201-5970)
