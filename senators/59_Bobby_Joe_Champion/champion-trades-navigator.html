Pipeline Explorer Six-stage pathway from pre-apprenticeship to journeyworker

In 2025, Senator Champion secured a $400,000 MAD DADS grant to train North Minneapolis youth ages 16 to 24 in HVAC, energy efficiency, and solar installation. That training is the door into the trades. This tool maps what comes after — the steps from a completed pre-apprenticeship program to a registered apprenticeship and, eventually, journeyworker status in a skilled trade.

Stage 1 — Pre-Apprenticeship Training

What it is: Short-cycle vocational training, typically 8–16 weeks. Programs in the Minnesota context include the MAD DADS HVAC/Solar program, YouthBuild MN, IOCP North Side trades training, and Hennepin Technical College pre-apprenticeship cohorts.

Eligibility: Usually ages 16–24. No prior trade experience required. Often income-qualified or justice-involved friendly. GED or high school diploma sometimes required; some programs support concurrent credential completion.

Outputs: Certificate of completion; OSHA-10 safety credential (standard); basic trade tool familiarity; work-readiness documentation.

Typical duration: 8–16 weeks.

Cost to participant: Usually free — funded by grants (MAD DADS, WIOA), or state workforce dollars.

Coordinator Action Items
  • Confirm participant completes OSHA-10 certification before program exit.
  • Ensure a formal completion certificate is issued — this is a required document for Stage 2.
  • Begin conversation about Stage 2 documentation before training ends — do not wait until after completion day.
Stage 2 — Transition Documentation

What it is: The administrative preparation that makes a participant "apprenticeship-ready." This stage is where most conversions fail — not from lack of skills but from paperwork and awareness gaps.

Required documents:

  • Valid Minnesota ID or driver's license
  • Social Security card or work authorization documentation
  • OSHA-10 card (or higher certification)
  • Proof of pre-apprenticeship completion (certificate)
  • Trade-specific physical/drug screen documentation (varies by program)
  • References from program instructor
Coordinator Action Item — Trade Dossier

Help the participant compile a "trade dossier" — a physical folder or PDF package with all documents, organized and ready to submit to a JATC or employer program. This should be completed before Stage 3 outreach begins.

Stage 3 — Apprenticeship Program Selection

What it is: Identifying the specific registered apprenticeship program that matches the participant's trade training. All registered apprenticeship programs in Minnesota are registered with the Department of Labor and Industry (DLI), Apprenticeship Unit.

DLI Apprenticeship: dli.mn.gov/apprenticeship DEED Apprenticeship MN: mn.gov/deed/business/finding-workers/apprenticeships/

There are two tracks. Both are registered with DLI:

Track A — Joint (Union-Affiliated)

Administered by the JATC of the relevant international union local. Applications typically handled through the union hall or a dedicated JATC office.

  • Sheet Metal Workers Local 10 (HVAC)
  • IBEW Local 292 (electrical)
  • UA Local 15 (plumbing/pipefitting)

Acceptance is competitive; written test and interview typically required. For current contact info, use the DLI registered program directory — do not rely on direct phone numbers, which change.

Track B — Non-Joint (Employer-Sponsored)

An individual employer or employer association registers a program with DLI. The employer recruits and sponsors apprentices directly.

More common in solar, energy efficiency, and emerging green trades where union programs are less established. Entry may be faster than joint programs.

This is the key leverage point for staff to negotiate direct-entry pipelines with employers on behalf of pre-apprenticeship graduates.

RTI (Related Technical Instruction): Participants in registered programs typically enroll in RTI coursework through the Minnesota State Colleges and Universities (MnSCU) system — usually the community or technical college affiliated with their JATC.

Coordinator Action Items
  • Pull the current DLI program directory for the participant's trade and geographic area: dli.mn.gov/apprenticeship.
  • Identify whether a joint or non-joint pathway is the better fit based on the participant's trade training and timeline.
  • If pursuing a non-joint employer pathway: contact the employer directly to inquire about pre-apprenticeship referral pipelines.
Stage 4 — Application and Selection

Joint programs — common requirements:

  • Written aptitude test (math and reading, typically NJATC standards)
  • Mechanical aptitude assessment
  • In-person interview
  • Physical requirements verification
  • Ranking on a waiting list by score

Non-joint programs — common requirements:

  • Employer interview
  • Background check policy varies by employer
  • Some programs accept direct referrals from pre-apprenticeship programs — the key leverage point for coordinators

Wait times: Union joint programs in metro trades can have 3–18 month waiting lists depending on trade and cohort timing. Non-joint programs may have faster entry. Set participant expectations clearly at this stage.

Coordinator Action Items
  • Follow up on application status at regular intervals — do not leave participants to track this alone.
  • Prepare participant for aptitude test: basic math review resources are available through most pre-apprenticeship programs.
  • Connect with any pre-apprenticeship program alumni who are already in the program — peer mentors are the most effective conversion tool.
  • Document direct-referral agreements with non-joint employers as a replicable program asset.
Stage 5 — Registered Apprenticeship (Active)

What it is: The formal apprenticeship. Typically 3–5 years. Combines on-the-job training (OJT) hours with related technical instruction (RTI) — usually evening classes at a community or technical college.

DLI tracking: Registered programs must track and report OJT hours to DLI. The apprentice accumulates hours and advances through wage periods.

Wage progression: Wages advance as a percentage of journeyworker scale at each interval. See the Wage Calculator module for trade-specific projections.

Key milestones:

  • Period completion reviews
  • RTI course completions at affiliated MnSCU institution
  • DLI-issued Certificate of Completion of Apprenticeship upon finishing all hours and RTI

Employer subsidy note: Employers may receive a subsidy of up to $3.50/hour for registered apprentices through DEED's Apprenticeship Minnesota program. This is a recruitment tool for coordinators working with non-joint employers. Verify current rate at mn.gov/deed.

Coordinator Action Items
  • Maintain contact with active apprentices — attrition risk is highest in Period 1 when wages are lowest relative to alternatives.
  • Ensure participant is enrolled in RTI coursework at their affiliated MnSCU institution.
  • Flag the $3.50/hr DEED employer subsidy when recruiting non-joint employer partners.
Stage 6 — Journeyworker Certification

What it is: Completion of all required OJT hours and RTI. DLI issues Certificate of Completion of Apprenticeship. In some trades, state licensing is also required — separate application through DLI with exam.

Trade MN State License Required? Exam / Additional Credential
Electrical Yes — MN Journeyworker Electrical License (DLI Electrical Licensing Unit) PSI exam required after apprenticeship completion
Plumbing / Pipefitting Yes — MN Journeyworker Plumber License (DLI) Exam required
HVAC / Refrigeration Yes — MN Refrigeration License (DLI) for refrigerant work EPA 608 certification required for refrigerant handling (federal requirement)
Solar PV Installation No separate MN solar journeyworker license; work falls under electrical licensing NABCEP certification — voluntary but industry-valued
General Construction / Energy Efficiency No separate journeyworker license (beyond electrical/plumbing if those systems involved) OSHA-30 is a common employer requirement at journeyworker level

Earnings at journeyworker: See Module 2 (Wage Calculator) for Minneapolis–St. Paul MSA wage ranges by trade, sourced from BLS OES data.

Coordinator Action Items
  • Verify trade-specific licensing exam requirements with DLI at least 6 months before apprenticeship completion so the participant has time to prepare.
  • Confirm RTI credit completion at MnSCU institution is fully documented and submitted to DLI.
