The Minnesota Sentencing Guidelines Commission was established by the Legislature in 1978 and began issuing guidelines in 1980. Minnesota was one of the first states to adopt a determinate sentencing grid.

The Standard Grid covers felony offenses other than sex offenses and drug offenses, which have separate grids with different severity levels and scoring.

As of 2025, the commission has 11 members appointed by the Governor.

Presumptive sentences are not mandatory. Judges may depart upward (aggravated) or downward (mitigated) with written findings. Appellate courts review departure decisions.

Stayed sentence: the prison term is suspended; the defendant serves probation. Executed sentence: the defendant serves prison time.

Minnesota uses "truth in sentencing": offenders serve the full executed sentence minus time served, with supervised release (not discretionary parole) afterward.
