2 MN Bonding Timeline Tracker
7 MILESTONES
April (odd year)
MMB Issues Capital Budget Instructions
Minnesota Management & Budget sends capital budget instructions to state agencies. Signals the start of the bonding cycle.
Responsible: MMB / State agencies
June 15 (odd year) — DEADLINE
Local Government Submission Deadline
City council resolution, project narrative, rationale, and local contribution letter must all be submitted. This is the most critical deadline for local governments.
Responsible: City / City Administrator
August (odd year)
Agency Compilation & MMB Submission
State agencies compile local requests and submit consolidated capital budget requests to MMB.
Responsible: State agencies / MMB
January (even year)
Governor Submits Capital Budget
Governor's capital budget recommendation goes to the Legislature. Your project may or may not be included.
Responsible: Governor's office
February – May (even year)
Legislative Bonding Bill Hearings
Capital Investment committees in both chambers hold hearings. Legislators advocate for their district projects. Testimony may be requested.
Responsible: Legislator / City (testimony)
May (even year)
Floor Votes — 3/5 Supermajority Required
Bonding bill requires a three-fifths supermajority in both the House and Senate. This high threshold means many projects fail — and a failed bill means a 2-year wait.
Responsible: Legislature
Summer (even year)
Governor Signs; MMB Releases Bond Proceeds
Governor signs or vetoes the bonding bill. If signed, MMB releases bond proceeds according to the bill's conditions.
Responsible: Governor / MMB
Note: MN Legislature typically passes bonding bills every other year. A failed bill means a 2-year wait.
June 15 Submission Package Checklist
  • City council resolution authorizing bond request (with specific dollar amount)
  • Project description (2–3 paragraphs: what, why, scope)
  • Community need narrative (population served, age of infrastructure, failure risk)
  • Local contribution statement (cash, in-kind, federal leveraging)
  • Project readiness statement (engineering complete? permits secured?)
  • Operating cost impact analysis (how does this affect future utility rates?)
  • Map or photos of the project area
Check items as you complete them. Progress persists on page reload via localStorage.

Red Lake Falls — Overlapping Capital Needs
In addition to the $5,947,000 state bond request (SF630, 2025) for sanitary sewer, storm sewer, streets, and sidewalk/curb/gutter, Red Lake Falls has a separate water tower project funded by a $906,000 USDA grant and $8.31M USDA loan. The city implemented 3% water/sewer rate increases in January 2026 for debt service on the water tower project alone. Multiple overlapping capital needs are typical for SD-1 communities.
