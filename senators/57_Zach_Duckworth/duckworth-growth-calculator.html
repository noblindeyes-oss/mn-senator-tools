Policy Context

Why Growth Management Math Matters in District 57

Lakeville is one of the fastest-growing cities in Minnesota — ninth-largest by population, with 24% growth between 2010 and 2020. That growth is the reason the Legislature allocated $5.4 million in SF24 to acquire land for a new water treatment facility and fire station: the city's existing infrastructure was sized for a smaller community.

Lakeville Area Schools (ISD 194) serves more than 12,000 students and is currently conducting attendance area adjustments and a school referendum as enrollment from new development continues to arrive.

Fast-growing suburbs face a timing mismatch: property tax revenue from new homes takes years to compound, but schools need seats and fire stations need land on day one. This tool helps quantify that gap.

About the School Funding Formula

Minnesota funds schools through a per-pupil formula. The basic formula allowance for FY2024 is $7,224 per pupil. This state aid travels with the student — when a new family moves into a district, the district receives additional state aid for their children. However, state aid covers only a portion of the actual per-pupil operating cost; the remainder is funded through local property tax levies. In a fast-growing district, new students arrive faster than the property tax base matures.

About Water Infrastructure

Minnesota cities are responsible for maintaining and expanding public water and sewer systems to serve new development. Connection fees partially offset new infrastructure costs, but large facilities — treatment plants, elevated storage, interceptor sewers — require debt financing spread across the entire rate base.

About Fire Station Coverage

Standard suburban fire response time goals are 4 minutes from dispatch to arrival. As cities expand geographically, new subdivisions at the urban edge may fall outside existing station coverage areas. Land for new fire stations must be acquired at the planning stage — not after the neighborhood is built.
