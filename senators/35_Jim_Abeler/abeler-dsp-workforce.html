40-Hour MN DHS Orientation Training

▶
What It Is
Required orientation training for all DSP workers in Minnesota. Mandated by MN DHS for staff working in licensed settings.
Issuing Body
Minnesota Department of Human Services
Time to Complete
40 hours (typically completed within first weeks of employment)
Cost to Worker
Generally employer-paid; voluntary orientation stipend of $100 available (MN SEIU/state contract)
Relevance to SF1062
Baseline requirement; the certification pilot would build upon this foundation.

College of Direct Support (CDS) Certificate

▶
What It Is
Online curriculum covering core DSP competency areas. Offered through the Institute on Community Integration at the University of Minnesota and licensed nationally.
Issuing Body
College of Direct Support / ICI at UMN (via directcourse.com)
Competency Areas
Community living, person-centered supports, communication, health and wellness, documentation, professional ethics
Approximate Cost
~$150/employee for employer-purchased access (often employer-paid); training completion stipend of $750 may be available (MN SEIU/state contract)
Wage Impact
CDS completion is a foundation for NADSP credentialing; certified DSPs earn an estimated $1-$3/hour more than non-certified peers at the same experience level (CQL/Anne Carlsen Center data).

NADSP DSP-I (Entry-Level National Credential)

▶
What It Is
Entry-level national credential recognizing demonstrated competency in direct support work.
Issuing Body
National Alliance for Direct Support Professionals (nadsp.org)
Requirements
Competency area documentation required; portfolio-based evidence of skill application.
Approximate Cost
~$100 application fee (verify current fee at nadsp.org); employer may cover
Wage Impact
Certified DSPs earn an estimated $1-$3/hour more than non-certified peers (CQL data).
Relevance to SF1062
SF1062's certification pilot would formalize recognition of NADSP credentials within Minnesota's service system.

Additional competency documentation; expanded portfolio demonstrating deeper skill application.

Within the $1-$3/hour premium range; specific tier differentiation varies by employer.

What It Is
Advanced national credential; the highest level of DSP professional recognition through NADSP.

NADSP

What SF1062 Proposes

Filed February 4, 2025, by Senator Jim Abeler (SD-35), SF1062 would establish a direct support professional certification pilot project and require a legislative report on outcomes.

It is a concrete, testable approach: create a certification framework for DSPs, pilot it in select settings, and report back to the Legislature on whether credentialing improves recruitment, retention, and service quality. Not a mandate. A pilot.

How Certification Addresses Turnover

Workers who can see a career structure stay longer. Organizations that have implemented NADSP credentialing have seen dramatic retention improvements.

Wage Context

Minnesota CNA median wage: $20.65/hr (BLS May 2023). Minnesota DSP median wage: $16.22/hr (MN DHS 2024-25). DSPs provide comparable direct care but earn significantly less, contributing to recruitment challenges.

The District Connection

SD-35 sits in Anoka County's outer-ring suburbs: Coon Rapids, Andover, Ramsey, and part of Anoka. These working-class and middle-class communities are heavily served by home and community-based providers — group homes, in-home services, county human services offices.

Anoka County has a large population of working-age adults with disabilities receiving waiver services. Workforce shortages directly affect whether those residents can stay in community settings or face institutional alternatives.
