Primary vs. Secondary MCL. The EPA sets two kinds of maximum contaminant levels. A Primary MCL is federally enforceable — water systems that exceed it must take corrective action and issue public notice. A Secondary MCL (SMCL) is a non-enforceable aesthetic guideline addressing taste, odor, or appearance. Manganese at 0.05 mg/L is a secondary standard; combined radium at 5 pCi/L is a primary standard. The regulatory weight is very different.

Why these contaminants appear in upper Midwest groundwater. Glacial aquifers across Minnesota contain naturally elevated levels of manganese and radium. Manganese dissolves from subsurface mineral deposits into groundwater under low-oxygen conditions common in deep wells. Radium-226 and radium-228 occur naturally in sandstone and granite formations; glacial aquifer geology in Wright County and surrounding areas is particularly associated with elevated radium levels.

SF2138 and the regional system. Senator Lucero's SF2138 (2025) appropriates $10 million through the Public Facilities Authority to the Albertville-Hanover-St. Michael joint powers water board. The investment addresses both capacity expansion (10 to 15 MGD) and installation of treatment equipment for manganese and radium — tackling the two problems simultaneously.

Minnesota Health Advisory. The Minnesota Department of Health sets an advisory level for manganese at 0.1 mg/L, above the federal SMCL of 0.05 mg/L. MDH recommends that infants under 1 year not consume water with manganese above 0.1 mg/L due to potential neurological effects at elevated exposure levels (above 0.3 mg/L).

Financing Context

Click text below to edit before printing.
The Minnesota Public Facilities Authority administers the Drinking Water Revolving Fund, which is the standard financing pathway for municipal water system upgrades. Systems seeking major capacity expansions may also pursue state bonding appropriations through the Capital Investment process. SF2138 (2025), authored by Senator Eric Lucero, is an example of this pathway — a $10,000,000 bond appropriation to the Public Facilities Authority for a grant to the joint powers water board serving Albertville, Hanover, and St. Michael.
