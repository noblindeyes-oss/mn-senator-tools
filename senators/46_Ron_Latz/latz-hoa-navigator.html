What Does Your Association Actually Owe You?

Thousands of Minnesotans live under homeowners association or condo rules that affect their home, their money, and their daily life. When something goes wrong -- a disputed assessment, a board that won't respond, a rule change that wasn't voted on properly -- it can be hard to know what the law actually requires. This tool walks you through Chapter 515B of Minnesota law, helps you identify what your association owes you, and gives you the written documentation to ask for it. Key Facts: MN CIC Law

  • Minnesota CIC law is Chapter 515B of Minnesota Statutes (1993, as amended)
  • The statute covers condominiums, cooperatives, and planned communities
  • All common interest communities created after June 1, 1994 are covered
  • Communities created before 1994 may have elected coverage; check your declaration
  • Governing documents can grant MORE rights than the statute but cannot take away statutory rights
  • The association board is a fiduciary -- it must act in the interest of all owners, not just current board members
  • Attorney fees may be awarded to a prevailing unit owner under Section 515B.4-116
