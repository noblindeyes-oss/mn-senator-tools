    R Policy Reference
    Ability-to-Pay Cap
    Under MnDOT's updated Cost Participation Policy (effective 2025-2026), local government cost responsibility for trunk-highway-eligible items on MnDOT-initiated cooperative construction projects is capped at 0.8% of the community's five-year average adjusted net tax capacity. Source: MnDOT Cost Participation Policy update, mandated by 2025 MN Transportation Budget (Line 128.21), authored in the Senate by Sen. John Jasinski (R-Faribault) and Sen. Scott Dibble (DFL-Minneapolis).
    Utility Relocation Reimbursement
    The updated policy directs MnDOT to compensate local governments from the Trunk Highway Fund for the remaining service life of city-owned utilities relocated due to trunk highway construction. League of Minnesota Cities representatives described these changes as "a game changer" for small and mid-size cities.

    What Is ANTC?
    Adjusted Net Tax Capacity (ANTC) is the total taxable value of all property in a jurisdiction after applying state-mandated class rate adjustments. Your county assessor publishes annual ANTC figures. For the ability-to-pay cap, MnDOT uses the five-year rolling average.
    What the Cap Does NOT Cover
    The cap applies to trunk-highway-eligible work on MnDOT-initiated projects. It does not apply to: (1) betterments — improvements a city requests beyond MnDOT's base project scope; (2) purely local street projects where MnDOT is not lead agency; (3) city-requested signal upgrades added to a MnDOT project.
