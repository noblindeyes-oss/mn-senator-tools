Why This Matters

Peer support programs are the evidence-based model for reducing suicide and behavioral health crises in first responder agencies. Most small and midsize Minnesota departments have no structured program. An informal check-in culture is not enough. What works is a trained team, a written policy, a referral pathway, and leadership that models help-seeking behavior.

Minnesota Statutes § 181.973 establishes confidentiality protections for peer support communications involving peace officers. Your legal counsel can advise on the specific application of these protections to your agency.
