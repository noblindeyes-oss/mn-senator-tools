Community Development Block Grant (CDBG)

Administered byIRRRB / DEED (federal pass-through)
DescriptionFederal CDBG funds passed through IRRRB to Iron Range local governments. Supports public infrastructure, housing rehabilitation, and economic development.
Eligible applicantsCities and townships in the IRRRB service area
Typical award$100,000 – $750,000 (varies by project type and federal allocation)
Match requirementGenerally no required local match for infrastructure; economic development projects may require local participation
Key requirementsMust primarily benefit low/moderate income persons (LMI test); Environmental Review required; Davis-Bacon prevailing wage applies to construction
TimelineApplications accepted on rolling basis; contact IRRRB for current cycles
ContactIRRRB Community Development division; 800-657-3775
  • Document the LMI percentage in your project's benefit area before starting the application. Census data or a local income survey can establish this.
  • Budget for Davis-Bacon wage compliance on any construction component. This is a federal requirement and cannot be waived.
  • Start the Environmental Review early; it can take 30-60 days and must be complete before funds are committed.
  • If combining CDBG with other IRRRB funds, note this in your application. Leveraging multiple sources strengthens the project.

IRRRB Infrastructure Grant Program (Public Works)

Funded byTaconite production tax revenues
DescriptionState-funded grants for public infrastructure in the Iron Range tax relief area.
Eligible applicantsLocal governments and Tribal governments in the IRRRB service area
Typical awardUp to $500,000 (larger projects may require additional justification)
Match requirementTypically 50% local match required; exceptions for financially distressed communities
Eligible projectsWater and sewer systems, roads, bridges, public buildings, parks
Key requirementsProject must serve a public benefit; must demonstrate community need; engineering feasibility preferred at application stage
ContactIRRRB Infrastructure/Community Grants; 800-657-3775
  • A preliminary engineering report greatly strengthens your application. Even a brief scope-and-cost letter from a licensed engineer helps.
  • Quantify the community need: how many residents are served, how old is the failing infrastructure, what happens if nothing is done.
  • If your community qualifies as financially distressed, document it. The match requirement may be reduced or waived.
  • Coordinate early with the IRRRB program officer. They can advise on project scope before you invest in a full engineering study.
  • When Cleveland-Cliffs production cuts reduce local tax base, that is relevant context for demonstrating community financial distress.

Business Development Loan/Grant Program

DescriptionLow-interest loans and grants to attract and retain businesses in the Iron Range, supporting economic diversification beyond mining.
Eligible applicantsPrivate businesses, developers, cooperatives locating or expanding in the IRRRB service area
Typical awardLoans $25,000 – $1,000,000+; grants typically smaller, tied to job creation
Match requirementBorrower equity contribution required (typically 10–20%)
Key requirementsMust create or retain permanent full-time jobs; business plan required; preference for projects that diversify the regional economy
ContactIRRRB Business Development; 800-657-3775
  • Job creation is the key metric. Be specific: how many positions, what wages, full-time or part-time, when will they be filled.
  • Projects that diversify the Iron Range economy beyond mining get preference. If your business brings a new industry sector, say so explicitly.
  • Prepare 2-3 years of financials or, for startups, detailed projections with assumptions documented.
  • Personal guarantees from owners are typically required for loan components. Discuss this with your lender and attorney early.

Workforce Development and Training Grants

DescriptionGrants to support job training, apprenticeships, and workforce readiness programs serving Iron Range residents.
Eligible applicantsSchool districts, community colleges, nonprofits, workforce training organizations
Typical award$10,000 – $150,000
Match requirementVaries; often no match required for nonprofit/school applicants
Key requirementsMust serve Iron Range residents; programs must lead to employment or credential attainment; reporting on outcomes required
ContextWith Iron Range school districts facing budget pressure — Hibbing cut 21 positions in 2025 to address a $2.8M shortfall — this program can fund career/technical education that the general fund cannot cover.
ContactIRRRB Workforce Development; 800-657-3775
  • Letters of support from local employers who will hire program graduates are among the strongest evidence you can provide.
  • Define measurable outcomes: how many people will be trained, what credential will they earn, what is the expected placement rate.
  • If your school district has faced budget cuts, document the gap. Show that this program fills a need the general fund cannot meet.
  • Partner with a community college or training provider if your organization does not deliver instruction directly.

Tourism and Recreation Development Grants

DescriptionGrants for tourism infrastructure, trail development, visitor amenities, and cultural/heritage attractions in the Iron Range.
Eligible applicantsLocal governments, nonprofits, Tribal governments
Typical award$10,000 – $200,000
Match requirementTypically 50% match (cash or in-kind)
Eligible projectsTrail construction/extension, signage, visitor centers, heritage sites, recreation facilities, tourism marketing with physical infrastructure component
ContactIRRRB Tourism/Recreation; 800-657-3775
  • Heritage preservation projects are strong candidates. For example, the Aitkin County Historical Society building at 20 Pacific Street, Aitkin — subject of Farnsworth's SF2226 ($100,000 roof appropriation request) — is exactly the kind of community asset this program exists to support.
  • Quantify tourism impact: visitor counts, overnight stays, spending estimates. Regional tourism data from Explore Minnesota can help.
  • In-kind match counts. Volunteer labor, donated materials, and staff time can all be documented as match.
  • Trail projects that connect to existing trail systems or regional networks are especially competitive.

Renewable Energy and Environmental Program

DescriptionGrants supporting renewable energy installations, energy efficiency improvements, and environmental remediation on Iron Range lands.
Eligible applicantsLocal governments, nonprofits, Tribal governments, in some cases private businesses
Typical award$25,000 – $300,000
Match requirementTypically 50% match; exceptions for environmental remediation
ContextThe Iron Range has significant environmental legacy from mining operations. This program supports brownfield/mine-land reuse projects.
ContactIRRRB Environmental; 800-657-3775
  • Mine-land remediation and reuse projects have strong justification built in. Document the environmental legacy and the community benefit of reclamation.
  • Energy efficiency projects for public buildings can show clear cost savings. Calculate the payback period and include it.
  • If combining with federal environmental grants (EPA Brownfields, etc.), note this as leveraged funding.
  • For renewable energy installations, include projected energy output and cost offset over the system lifetime.
