Prior Authorization Appeal Guide

"Don't Accept the First Denial"

When a health insurance company denies a treatment your doctor has ordered, that isn't necessarily the end.

Minnesota law gives you the right to appeal — and a well-prepared appeal succeeds more often than most people expect.

This tool walks you through the process step by step: understanding what happened, knowing your deadlines, and writing a clear appeal letter.

It takes about 10 minutes. You don't need a lawyer.

In December 2025, the National Council of Insurance Legislators — the national organization of state insurance lawmakers — adopted a Prior Authorization Reform Model Act designed to speed up insurer decisions, require transparency about denial criteria, and protect patients' relationships with their doctors. Minnesota's own reform law took effect in 2026. This tool reflects the rights you have under current Minnesota law and federal rules.

Know Your Rights in Minnesota

Minn. Stat. 62M.05 Utilization Review: insurers must disclose the clinical criteria used in any adverse determination, within 5 business days of request.
Minn. Stat. 62Q.68 Grievance Procedures: internal appeal decision must be made within 30 days (or 72 hours if urgent).
Minn. Stat. 62Q.73 External Review: patient may request IRO review within 4 months of final internal adverse determination; the IRO decision is binding on the insurer.
Federal MHPAEA Mental health / substance use PA criteria must be no more restrictive than medical/surgical criteria for the same plan.
No Surprises Act (2022) For out-of-network emergency care, certain balance billing protections apply separately from PA rules.
