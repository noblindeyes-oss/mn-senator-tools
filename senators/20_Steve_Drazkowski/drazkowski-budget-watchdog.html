Local Context
SF618 (Drazkowski, 2023): $2,894,000 appropriation for Trunk Highway 60 realignment in Wabasha — local roads, sidewalks, sewer, water main, storm sewer, lighting, and pedestrian safety. Part of a $9.9M total project ($5M federal FY2024, Klobuchar/Smith; $4.9M requested state bonding; construction projected 2027 if funded). Even while pressing for statewide fiscal restraint, Drazkowski carried this targeted infrastructure investment for his district.

Surplus Record
Minnesota ran surpluses of ~$7.7 billion (FY2021-22) and ~$17.6 billion (projected peak before FY2023-24 session) before a large portion was spent. Drazkowski's repeated position: surplus money should be returned to taxpayers, not spent. The FY2025-26 enacted budget projects ~$66.0B — and a looming $2.31 billion deficit.

Health & Human Services now accounts for more than 37% of Minnesota's all-funds budget — the largest single category. It has grown faster than inflation and population combined in every biennium since 2013.

SF2101 Context
Drazkowski authored SF2101 to repeal inflation adjustments on the Child Tax Credit and Working Family Credit — arguing that refundable credits function as spending programs embedded in the tax code, not as true tax relief. The HHS and E-12 budget lines above include the fiscal impact of such credits.
