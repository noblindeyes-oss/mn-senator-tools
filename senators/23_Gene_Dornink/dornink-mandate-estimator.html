Sen. Dornink's core concern with these mandates is the absence of differentiation between large corporations and small employers, seasonal farm operations, and micro-businesses. His public record shows consistent advocacy for flexibility provisions — allowing employers with part-time, seasonal, or low-hour workers to structure benefits differently from those designed for year-round, full-time workforces. The data above reflects the laws as currently enacted. His legislative alternative proposals have focused on small employer carve-outs, flexible accrual systems, and removal of one-size-fits-all enforcement frameworks.
