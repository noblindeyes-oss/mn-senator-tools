Infrastructure

Senator Clark's SF2286 changes what Minneapolis must allow on commercial corridors -- and SF3567 funds the storm sewer infrastructure that makes that density buildable. The rules in SF2286 are dense. This tool translates them into a plain-language compliance check.

SF3567: $25 million state bond for 2,100 feet of new storm sewer under 13th Avenue NE, from 2nd Street NE to a Mississippi River outfall. Storm sewer capacity is infrastructure that makes dense housing viable. SF3567 addresses the physical infrastructure; SF2286 addresses the legal framework.
SF2286 Unit Threshold
13
Minimum residential units for SF2286 protections to apply in a single residential building on a commercial corridor.
Step 01
Zoning Classification
How to find your zoning: Go to Minneapolis Maps (minneapolismn.gov/maps), search your address, and look for the Zoning layer. Commercial zones in Minneapolis are labeled C1, C2, C3, C3A, or B4. If your parcel is in one of those zones, answer YES above.
SF2286 applies to commercial zones only. If the zone also allows heavy industrial as a principal use, the bill's protections do not apply to that zone.
Step 02
Project Size
SF2286 protections kick in for buildings with at least 13 units, or mixed-use buildings with commercial on the ground floor meeting that threshold. Projects under 13 units in a purely residential configuration are not covered.
Step 03
Location
Minneapolis is within the Twin Cities metro area. If your parcel is in Minneapolis, answer Yes.
