The MPSP operates under an ACA Section 1332 State Innovation Waiver. SF333 requires a waiver renewal application by December 31, 2026, for benefit years beginning January 1, 2028.

Why It Matters in Southwest Minnesota

Southwest Minnesota has a higher proportion of self-employed farmers, agricultural workers, and small business owners than the state average. These are the households most likely to purchase coverage on the individual market — without an employer subsidizing premiums. When individual-market premiums spike, farmers and rural business owners feel it directly. The reinsurance program was specifically designed to protect this segment of the market.

Without SF333 passing, an estimated 93,000 Minnesotans would have lost affordable coverage statewide. In District 15 — Brown, Lac qui Parle, Lyon, Redwood, and Yellow Medicine counties — the impact would have been concentrated among the self-employed and farm families who have no other source of coverage.

Insurance Terms in Plain English

Premium
The monthly payment to keep your insurance active, whether you use it or not.
Deductible
The amount you pay out of your own pocket before insurance starts covering most costs.
Out-of-Pocket Maximum
The most you will ever pay in a single year; after this, insurance covers 100%.
Reinsurance
Insurance for insurance companies — it protects them against catastrophically expensive patients so they can charge everyone else less.
Individual Market
Health insurance you buy yourself, not through an employer — common for farmers, freelancers, and small business owners.
MNsure
Minnesota's state health insurance marketplace where you can shop for individual and family plans and apply for financial assistance.
Premium Tax Credit
A federal discount that reduces your monthly premium based on your income level.
Metal Tiers (Bronze / Silver / Gold / Platinum)
Plan categories defined by how costs are split — Bronze has the lowest premiums but highest out-of-pocket costs; Platinum is the reverse.
HSA (Health Savings Account)
A tax-advantaged savings account for medical expenses, available with certain high-deductible plans. 2025 limits: $4,300 individual / $8,550 family.
COBRA
Temporary continuation of employer health coverage after leaving a job — typically available for 18 months, often expensive.
