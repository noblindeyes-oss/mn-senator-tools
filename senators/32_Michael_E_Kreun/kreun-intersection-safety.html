Senator Kreun's SF4789 secured $4.3 million to convert the TH 65 and Bunker Lake Boulevard intersection in Ham Lake to a grade-separated interchange. Dozens of other at-grade intersections across north Anoka County face the same congestion and safety pressure. The federal Highway Safety Improvement Program (HSIP) funds safety projects at 90-100% federal share, with approximately $33 million available statewide annually -- 60% reserved for local governments. This tool helps city engineers and county planners build the case.
TH 65 Corridor Context -- Senate District 32

Minnesota State Highway 65 is a principal arterial running from I-694 in Fridley through Blaine and Ham Lake northward. It carries four lanes through Anoka County as an expressway before transitioning to a two-lane rural highway north of Cambridge. The suburban segment through District 32 is among the highest-volume sections of the corridor and is subject to ongoing growth pressure from residential and commercial development in Blaine (70,000+ residents) and Ham Lake.

SF4789, chief-authored by Sen. Kreun, appropriated $4,300,000 for interchange improvements at the TH 65 / Bunker Lake Boulevard intersection in 2024, covering engineering, environmental review, right-of-way acquisition, and construction.

EPDO = (K x 500) + (A x 100) + (B x 30) + (C x 10) + (O x 1)
EPDO Rate = (EPDO x 1,000,000) / (AADT_major + AADT_minor) / 365 / Years
